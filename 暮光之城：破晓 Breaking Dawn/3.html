Chapter 2 Long Night

"I miss you already.""I don't need to leave. I can stay___""Mmm."It was quiet for a long moment, just the thud of my heart hammering, the broken rhythm of our raggedbreathing, and the whisper of our lips moving in synchronization.

　　Sometimes it was so easy to forget that I was kissing a vampire. Not because he seemed ordinary orhuman—I could never for a second forget that I was holding someone more angel than man in myarms—but because he made it seem like nothing at all to have his lips against my lips, my face, my throat.

He had the most beautiful soul, more beautiful than his brilliant mind or hisincomparable face or his glorious body.

Who knew why—somestrange glitch in my brain that made it immune to all the extraordinary and frightening things someimmortals could do. 　　My last night in my room. My last night as Isabella Swan. Tomorrow night, I would be Bella Cullen.

Once upon a time, before I was born probably, she'd made her play for Edward—not that I blamedher or anyone else for wanting him.
